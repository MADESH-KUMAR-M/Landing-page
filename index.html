Explore Beauty of Delhi

India Gate

India Gate is a 42-meter high arch made of red sandstone and granite, designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens. The names of 13,500 soldiers who were killed in World war are inscribed on the walls of this structure. The running fountains and well-manicured lawns around the memorial make it a breathtaking place for a picnic as well as a family outing.

Red fort

The Red Fort, built in the 17th century, is an impressive example of Mughal architecture and is considered a symbol of Indian independence since the end of British rule. The Red Fort in Delhi is a famous and historic monument that was once the residence of the Mughal emperors. It is a must-visit attraction in India. Visitors to the Red Fort can explore the fort's defenses, see the royal palace, and appreciate the fusion of Mughal, Persian, Timurid, and Hindu design elements.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is one of the most famous and beautiful buildings in the world. It is a white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India, on the bank of the Yamuna River. The construction of the Taj Mahal started in 1632 and took 16 years to complete. More than 20,000 workers, artisans, and craftsmen from India and other countries were involved in the project.
